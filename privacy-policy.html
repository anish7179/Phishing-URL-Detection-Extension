Privacy Policy for Phishing URL Detector

Last Updated: July 12, 2026

1. Introduction

Phishing URL Detector ("we", "our", or "the extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how our Chrome Extension handles your data. The core philosophy of this extension is privacy-first processing.

2. Data Collection and Usage

This extension operates by analyzing URLs you visit locally on your device to determine if they exhibit phishing characteristics. We do not collect, store, or transmit your browsing history to our own servers.

  • Local Processing: Machine-learning heuristics, entropy analysis, and typosquatting calculations are performed entirely on your device.
  • Local Storage: Your settings (such as whitelist data and sensitivity preferences) and historical threat detections are stored locally on your device using Chrome's local storage API. They are never synced to our servers.

3. Third-Party Services

To provide enhanced security, the extension may communicate with the following third-party services:

  • RDAP (WHOIS) Lookups: When checking domain age, the domain name (but not the full URL path) is sent to public RDAP servers (rdap.org) to fetch public registration dates.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, rent, or otherwise share your personal information or browsing data with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

5. Changes to this Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time in order to reflect changes to our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Any changes will be reflected with a revised "Last Updated" date.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions or suggestions regarding our Privacy Policy, please contact us via our GitHub repository: https://github.com/anish7179/Phishing-URL-Detection-Extension.
